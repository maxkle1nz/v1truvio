MASSIF — three divergent studies of the Vista Organismo

Same real data (4 containers by real node_count: entry_points·L3=82, data_access·L1=60, tests·L2=42, data_access·L0=15; the live graph is 100% unpainted). Three structurally different ways to compose the field, rendered before the pick — because judging them rendered can change the choice. The chosen one carries the three signature gestures at real density.

Study A — Stacked Strata: containers as horizontal geological bands stacked by layer (L0→L3), blocks flow inside. Reads like a rock-face cross-section. Rejected: honest to "layers" but forces equal band heights that FIGHT node_count (a 15-node band and an 82-node band look the same weight) — Lissitzky's "weight = count" law dies, and density-7 gets cramped into thin rows.
Study B — Squarified Quarter: the true squarified treemap the technical constraint names — plates sized by node_count, blocks tiled inside, isometric. Reads as aerial territory. CHOSEN: weight=count is structural (Lissitzky), it packs the most blocks legibly side-by-side (the density-7 + the pedestal-vs-grey distinction test), and the plate top face is the natural home of the STATE-STRATUM CAP.
Study C — Terrace Cascade: containers as isometric terraces at descending elevations. Reads like an excavation with levels. Rejected: beautiful depth, but elevation encodes NOTHING real (it's decorative profundidade — Rams cuts it), and terraces waste field area, hurting the density the diagnosis needs.
